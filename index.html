Jennifer
Whether it's your first time visiting or you already live here, Florida has incredible attractions that make it an ideal spot for a memorable summer vacation. From the bustling cities of Tampa, Orlando and Miami to serene retreats from coast to coast, a trip in this sun-kissed state is an experience like no other!

Living here myself, I've been all over. I've gone on camping trips in many of Florida's lush, green state parks, seen dolphins swimming along the panhandle's emerald waters, and experienced the vibrant city of Miami at night. But there's still so much more to see!

If you're a nature lover like me, the state offers over 700 freshwater springs, 825 miles of beaches, and more than 1,300 natural trails for a family-filled adventure. Saddle up in Ocala and ride horseback or zipline in the trees across Florida's luscious terrains.

Along our serene waterways, you'll glimpse into the side of nature you don't quite see anywhere else. You can kayak through mangroves and paddleboard among Manatees. Or, hop on a charter boat and grab the catch of the day in the World's Luckiest Fishing Village in Destin and Fort Walton Beach. There are over 20 edible game fish you can catch!

Beyond its outdoor fun, the Sunshine State offers plenty of exciting attractions that are inclusive of families with disabilities, too. Wonderworks in Orlando allows kiddos on the spectrum to explore the fascination of science with indoor play, while Zoo Tampa and Florida Aquarium in Tampa Bay bring on the spectacle of wildlife and autism-friendly exhibits to discover.

For history buffs with additional needs, The Cummer Museum in Jacksonville offers large print guides, pre-scheduled ASL services, and touch tours for those who are blind or have low vision. These are a few of the many attractions across the state that accommodate accessibility for visitors, with experiences that have no limit to the fun.

Plus, Florida loves your pets, too, and there are tons of hotels, restaurants and attractions that let them be a part of your special trip. They're a part of the family, after all!

If you want to experience something memorable with your family this summer, book your stay in The Sunshine State. To plan your Florida vacation and learn more, check out VISITFLORIDA.com. We hope to see you soon!
